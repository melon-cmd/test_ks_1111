## 風險與挑戰

Like any ambitious hardware project, creating Nosh One comes with its own set of challenges. Building a fully autonomous cooking robot means balancing robotics, food safety, AI, and manufacturing — no small feat. But we’ve spent years prototyping, testing, and setting up production to minimize surprises.

Some of the risks we are mindful of include:

Manufacturing & Supply Chain: Component shortages or supplier delays could impact timelines. We’ve partnered with reliable manufacturers and built backup options.

Scaling Production: The first few hundred units are always easier than the next thousand. We’re putting strong QA processes in place to maintain consistency.

Certifications & Safety: Food-grade and electrical certifications can sometimes take longer than planned, but all our materials and electronics are chosen with compliance in mind.

Kitchen Realities: Ingredients vary globally — rice in India doesn’t behave like rice in the US. Our Culinary Intelligence learns and adapts, but early versions may need fine-tuning for some dishes.

Global Logistics: Shipping a robot across countries has its quirks. We’re working with experienced partners to ensure safe, timely delivery.
