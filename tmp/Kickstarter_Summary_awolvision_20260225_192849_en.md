- [14_2] AWOL Vision
  - Hi Arnaud,
Thank you for reaching out. We fully respect the rigor of Greg‘s testing methodology, just as we are highly committed to the accuracy and integrity of our laboratory measurements.
As we know, there are two common methods for testing projector brightness: one is to measure lumens or lux directly from projector output, and the other is to use an optical probe to measure the reflected light from the screen in nits (which is the method you used). There is a well-established conversion formula between these methods:
Lumen=(Nit*Screen Square*3.14)/Screen Gain
The two main factors affecting the nit data based on Greg‘s test method:
1. The distance between the test probe and the screen, the greater the distance, the lower the nit data measurement will be.
2. The actual screen gain (which cannot simply be assumed to equal the manufacturer’s nominal gain; it should be measured directly).
We would suggest measuring brightness using lux values rather than nits. After the Chinese New Year holiday, we will arrange to send Greg a Konica Minolta T-10A professional illuminance meter as soon as possible to double-check the attached brightness measured data we did in our Lab.
